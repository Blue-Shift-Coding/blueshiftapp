Our Mission

Our aim is to enable and encourage children aged 5 to 14 to be digital creators and support their parents and teachers. We want to excite all children, whether they’re naturally inclined to learn to code or not, about the things they can make and do with code.

our values

We believe that if taught with a focus on creativity, learning to code will not only equip children with the technical skills they need to excel in the future, but will also teach them critical problem solving skills. There are many platforms on the market which advocate a very prescriptive approach to computer programming; however, without encouraging children to problem solve and realise their own ideas, they will never be properly equipped to work in a rapidly changing world which requires adaptive and innovative thinkers. We believe that children are most motivated to learn digital skills and code when those skills support something they want to make.

our services

blue{shift} is West London’s premier provider of coding and robotics instruction and teaching and assessment materials. We were started in 2013 with the aim of teaching children aged 5 to 14 a creative approach to coding. We run after-school clubs at a growing roster of schools, workshops and camps. Our courses have looked at everything from designing wearables to 3D printing to creating VR experiences with code.

meet the team

Heather Lyons

Founder & Director

Heather has worked in digital media since 1999. She was bitten by the tech bug early on and excitedly began building apps and websites as soon as she left school. Heather has taught computer-based design at the Architectural Association, has a Masters degree in Architecture from Princeton University and has done further advanced study in Computer Science at UCL. Heather also has 3 young children.

Jordan Wu

Content Technologist & Educator

Jordan Wu is a digital artist and has a BsC in Digital arts and Computing. He works as the Creative Technologist and Educator for blue{shift}. Jordan has a keen interest in virtual reality, developing new ways for immersive experiences, through programs and games.
